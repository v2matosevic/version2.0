# Brand Voice & Copy Guidelines

## The Tone

Version2's voice is what separates us from every other web dev studio with a dark theme and a "Let's build something" headline.

**We sound like:** A sharp friend who happens to be really good at building things for the web. Confident but not arrogant. Technical but not jargon-heavy. Direct but not cold.

**We don't sound like:** A corporate brochure. A LinkedIn post. A sales pitch. An AI-generated landing page.

## Rules

1. **Write like you talk.** If you wouldn't say it out loud, don't write it. No "leverage synergies" or "transform your digital presence." Just say what you mean.

2. **Don't sell. Talk.** The copy should feel like a conversation, not a pitch deck. We are not trying to convince anyone. We are telling them what we do and letting the work speak.

3. **Be specific, not vague.** "We built a member portal with Stripe billing, journal tracking, and role-based access" beats "We create innovative digital solutions."

4. **Slightly edgy.** We can have an opinion. We can say WordPress is slow. We can say templates look like templates. We are not trying to please everyone.

5. **Short sentences win.** Don't chain three clauses together. Break them apart. Let them breathe.

6. **No fluff words.** Banned: "innovative", "cutting-edge", "solutions", "leverage", "empower", "seamless", "transform", "elevate", "next-level", "holistic", "synergy", "robust."

7. **Show, don't tell.** Instead of "We build fast websites" show a metric. Instead of "We are experienced" show the work.

## Formatting

- Avoid em dashes (—) 
- Avoid semicolons and colons in copy where possible
- Prefer periods and commas
- Short paragraphs. 2-3 sentences max.
- Headlines should be punchy, not descriptive

## Language Priority

1. **English** — primary language. All copy is written in English first.
2. **Croatian** — secondary. Translated and adapted, not word-for-word.
3. **German** — tertiary. Translated for the Austrian/DACH market.

## Examples of What We Want

**Bad:** "We leverage cutting-edge technologies to deliver innovative digital solutions that transform your online presence."

**Good:** "We write code. You get a website. Customer gets you."

**Bad:** "Our team of experienced professionals is dedicated to providing seamless user experiences across all platforms."

**Good:** "Your visitors don't care about our process. They care that the page loads fast and looks good. So do we."

**Bad:** "Contact us today to schedule a consultation and discover how we can elevate your business."

**Good:** "Got a project? Let's talk."

## Headlines

Headlines should make someone stop scrolling. They should be short, opinionated, or surprising.

- Avoid: "Welcome to Version2" / "Your Digital Partner" / "Web Development Services"
- Prefer: Something that says who we are in a way nobody else would say it
