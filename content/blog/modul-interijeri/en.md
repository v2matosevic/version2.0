# Modul Interijeri: Complete Website Redesign for an Interior Design Brand

Interior design companies live and die by aesthetics. If your website doesn't look like you care about design, why would anyone trust you to design their space?

## The Client

Modul Interijeri is a Croatian interior design company specializing in residential and commercial projects. They create modern spaces that balance form and function. Clean lines, considered material choices, and a design philosophy rooted in how people actually live and work.

Their old website told a different story. It felt outdated, generic, and disconnected from the quality of their actual work. Potential clients landing on that site were getting the wrong first impression.

## The Challenge

The existing site had three problems.

First, it didn't reflect the brand. Modul Interijeri does modern, refined work. The website looked like it was built from a template in 2018, which it was. The visual gap between their physical portfolio and their digital presence was significant.

Second, search visibility was poor. Potential clients searching for interior design services in Croatia weren't finding them. The site had no SEO strategy, weak metadata, and a structure that search engines struggled to parse.

Third, the user experience was lacking. Navigation was confusing. Project imagery was small and poorly presented. There was no clear path from "this looks interesting" to "let me get in touch."

They needed a full redesign. Not a facelift. A rethinking of how the brand presents itself online.

## What We Built

We did a complete tear-down and rebuild. Every pixel, every word, every technical decision was made fresh.

### Dark, Sophisticated Design

The new [website](/services/web-design/) uses a refined dark color palette. Deep black backgrounds with carefully chosen accent colors let the project imagery pop. The contrast between the dark interface and the bright interior photography creates a gallery-like effect. Each project gets the visual weight it deserves.

The design is intentional. Every section serves a purpose. No filler content, no generic stock imagery. Just a clear presentation of who Modul Interijeri is and what they do. The visual language communicates the same thing their physical work does: precision, taste, and confidence.

### Project Showcase

Interior design is a visual discipline. The project pages were built to let the work speak for itself. Large images, minimal text interference, and smooth transitions between projects. Visitors can browse the full portfolio without friction.

We organized projects by type (residential, commercial) so potential clients can quickly find work relevant to their own needs. Each project page includes enough context to tell the story without overwhelming the imagery.

### Performance and SEO

On the technical side, we focused on speed and [SEO](/services/seo/). The site loads fast despite image-heavy pages, thanks to optimized WebP formats, lazy loading, and efficient code.

We rebuilt the site structure from scratch. Clean URLs, proper heading hierarchy, optimized metadata, and internal linking that helps both visitors and search engines understand the content. Fresh copywriting throughout the site reinforces the brand's positioning. The language is direct and confident, matching the design aesthetic.

### Responsive Experience

Every page adapts cleanly to mobile, tablet, and desktop. Interior design clients often browse on their phones. A project gallery that looks stunning on desktop but breaks on an iPhone is useless. We made sure [the mobile experience](/blog/importance-of-responsive-design-on-mobile-devices/) was just as considered as the desktop one.

## The Result

Modul Interijeri now has a website that feels like their work. Dark, modern, and intentional. Search visibility improved, and the site serves as a proper portfolio that attracts the right kind of client inquiries.

The gap between their physical work and their digital presence is gone. When a potential client visits modul-interijeri.hr, they immediately understand what this company is about.
