Receiving a campaign disapproval notification from Meta is frustrating for digital marketers. As a digital agency running hundreds of Facebook campaigns monthly, the Version2 team has developed systematic approaches to resolve these issues quickly.

#### Why Facebook Rejects Campaigns

Meta's rejection reasons typically include:

- **AI content detection**: Algorithms flag potentially problematic words like "problems," "struggle," or "solution"
- **Health and wellness restrictions**: Additional scrutiny for dietary supplements and fitness programs requiring special certifications
- **Landing page issues**: Pages lacking clear privacy policies, contact information, or SSL certificates fail review
- **Strict age verification**: Campaigns targeting 18+ audiences face rigorous examination

#### Initial Response Strategy

Don't make immediate changes when rejected. Instead:

1. Screenshot all notifications and campaign settings
2. Verify your account status isn't compromised
3. Review recent Meta policy updates

#### Systematic Troubleshooting Approach

**Content Analysis**
- Remove superlatives like "best," "revolutionary," or "guaranteed"
- Ensure visuals comply with text overlay restrictions where applicable
- Review call-to-action button language for compliance

**Audience Targeting Review**
- Audience size (minimum 1,000 people recommended)
- Health and age restriction combinations
- Custom Audience consent documentation

**Landing Page Verification**

Your destination must include:
- HTTPS security certificate
- Accessible privacy policy
- Contact information
- Mobile-responsive design

#### Contacting Meta Support Effectively

Provide specific information when appealing:
- Campaign ID and Business Manager ID
- Exact rejection reason quoted
- Explanation of policy compliance
- Evidence supporting legitimacy claims

#### Alternative Marketing Channels

While awaiting resolution:
- Test Google Ads campaigns
- Boost organic social content
- Activate email marketing initiatives

#### Prevention Strategies

**Best practices for future campaigns:**

- Test with modest budgets ($50-100) before full launch
- Use Facebook Creative Hub for pre-screening
- Document all advertising claims
- Avoid restricted terminology: "virus," "pandemic," "instant results," "100% guaranteed"

#### Key Takeaways

Most campaign rejections (approximately 90%) resolve through methodical investigation and patience. Meta's approval systems occasionally malfunction, and support exists to address legitimate concerns.
