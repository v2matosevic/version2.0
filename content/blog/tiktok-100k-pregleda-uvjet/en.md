# TikTok's 100k view requirement: why it's the key to making money

You've probably heard that TikTok pays creators, but then you came across that famous requirement – 100,000 views in the last 30 days. Sounds like an impossible mission? It's not that simple, but it's definitely achievable if you know what you're doing.

## What exactly does the 100k views requirement mean?

TikTok requires that your videos accumulate at least 100,000 total views in a 30-day period before you can enter the [Creator Rewards Program](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program). This isn't about one viral video – we're talking about the combined views of all your posts.

Let's say you have 10 videos that have gotten 10,000 views each, or maybe one hit with 80,000 views plus a few smaller ones with a total of 20,000. Everything counts.

## Why does TikTok even have this requirement?

TikTok is not a cheesy company that just wants to make life difficult for creators. This requirement serves as a filter for serious creators who can consistently produce content that audiences want to watch.

Platforms like TikTok make money from ads – the more people watch your content, the more they can charge advertisers. Discovering the business potential of TikTok only becomes a reality when you have an audience that returns regularly.

Additionally, this requirement helps eliminate spam accounts and those who try to "game" the system with fake reviews.

## Who can achieve this condition?

Almost anyone who takes their content creation seriously can reach 100k views in a month. Here's what you need:

**Consistency is key** – You can't post one video a week and expect miracles. Successful creators post every day or at least every other day.

**Knowing the audience** – Ideas for TikTok videos can help, but you need to understand what your specific audience wants to see.

**Optimization for the algorithm** – The TikTok algorithm favors videos that keep viewers engaged until the end. Tips for editing videos for TikTok can significantly improve your performance.

## Other monetization requirements

100k views is just one piece of the puzzle. TikTok also requires:

- At least 10,000 followers
- Age 18+
- Original content longer than one minute (for the Creator Rewards Program)
- Account in good standing with no community guidelines violations

## How much can you earn after you pass the requirements?

[According to the latest data](https://influencermarketinghub.com/how-much-does-tiktok-pay/), the Creator Rewards Program pays between €0.40 and €1.00 per 1,000 views – a significant improvement over the old Creator Fund which only paid €0.02-0.04.

This means that a video with a million views can bring in between €400 and €1,000 directly from TikTok. Add in brand sponsorships, affiliate marketing and your own products, and the numbers get more interesting.

## How to achieve 100k views in 30 days?

**Follow trends, but add your own spin** – How to make short viral videos shows the importance of a balance between popular trends and original creativity.

**Solve the problem** – The most successful TikTok videos solve a problem, teach something new, or entertain in a unique way.

**Optimize for retention** – If people don't watch your video to the end, the algorithm won't promote it. The first parts should intrigue viewers.

**Use analytics** – Monitor what works and what doesn't. Why your TikTok videos stop at around 250 views explains common problems.

## Alternative ways to earn money

Even if you don't reach 100k views right away, you can start earning through:

- Sponsored posts with brands
- Affiliate marketing
- Selling your own products or services
- TikTok Live gifts

## Realistic expectations

Achieving 100k views in a month is not impossible, but it does require strategy and persistence. Most successful creators need 3-6 months of consistent posting to reach this number.

Successfully using TikTok requires a combination of creativity, technical skills, and understanding of your audience. It's not just "publish and wait" – it's real work that can bring serious results.

The 100k view requirement may seem daunting, but it serves as proof that you are a serious creator ready to monetize. Instead of seeing it as an obstacle, treat it as the first real goal on the path to a successful TikTok career.
