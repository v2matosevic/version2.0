# Semrush: What It Is, What It Does, and Whether You Need It

If you've spent any time reading about [SEO](/blog/what-is-seo-optimization/), you've seen Semrush mentioned. It shows up in almost every "best SEO tools" list. Every SEO tutorial seems to use it for screenshots. And their affiliate program is so generous that half the internet is trying to sell you a subscription.

So let's cut through the noise. What does Semrush actually do? Is it worth the money? And do you even need it?

## What Semrush Actually Is

Semrush is a web-based platform that gives you data about your website's search engine performance, your competitors' strategies, and your market's keyword landscape.

That's it. No magic. No automatic rankings boost. It's a research and analytics tool. You still have to do the work. But having the right data makes that work dramatically more effective.

Think of it like this: doing SEO without a tool like Semrush is like trying to navigate a city without a map. You can wander around and eventually find your destination. But with a map, you take the fastest route and avoid the dead ends.

## The Core Features That Matter

Semrush has dozens of tools inside it. Most people use about five of them. Here are the ones that actually move the needle.

### Keyword Research

This is Semrush's bread and butter. You type in a topic, and it shows you what people actually search for, how often they search for it, how hard it would be to rank for it, and what the current top-ranking pages look like.

This is critical for [choosing the right keywords](/blog/keyword-research-for-web-design/). Without keyword data, you're guessing what your audience cares about. With it, you're building content around proven demand.

Semrush also shows keyword intent. Are people searching to buy something, learn something, or compare options? That distinction changes how you should create content for each keyword.

### Competitive Analysis

This is where Semrush gets interesting. You can plug in any competitor's domain and see what keywords they rank for, how much traffic they get, what their top pages are, and where their [backlinks](/blog/what-are-backlinks-why-are-important/) come from.

It's not perfect. The data is estimated, not exact. But the patterns are reliable enough to base strategy on. You can find gaps where competitors rank and you don't. You can spot keywords they're targeting that you haven't considered. You can reverse-engineer what's working for them.

This saves massive amounts of time. Instead of guessing what content to create, you can see exactly what's already working in your space and build something better.

### Site Audit

Semrush crawls your entire website and flags technical issues. Broken links. Missing meta descriptions. Slow pages. Duplicate content. Crawl errors. The stuff that silently kills your search rankings.

Most site owners have no idea these problems exist. A 20-minute audit can surface issues that have been dragging down your [site performance](/blog/how-to-optimize-web-page-speed/) for months.

The audit tool prioritizes issues by severity, so you know what to fix first. Critical errors that block indexing get flagged differently than minor warnings about image alt text.

### Backlink Analysis

Backlinks are still one of the strongest ranking factors. Semrush tracks your backlink profile, shows you new and lost links, and lets you analyze the quality of sites linking to you.

It also shows you where your competitors get their backlinks. This is gold for [building your own backlink strategy](/blog/how-can-get-more-backlinks/). If a site links to three of your competitors but not to you, that's a prospect worth pursuing.

You can also monitor your [domain rating](/blog/what-is-domain-rating/) over time and see how it compares to competitors in your space.

### Position Tracking

You pick your target keywords, and Semrush tracks where you rank for each one over time. Daily updates. Broken down by device and location.

This turns SEO from "I think we're doing well" into "we moved from position 14 to position 7 for this keyword in the last 30 days." Concrete data you can act on and report to stakeholders.

## What Semrush Does Not Do

Let's be clear about limitations.

Semrush does not do your SEO for you. It gives you data. You (or your SEO specialist) still need to interpret that data and take action.

Semrush traffic estimates are not exact numbers. They're estimates based on ranking positions and average click-through rates. Useful for comparisons and trends, not for precise traffic counts.

Semrush does not guarantee results. A tool is only as good as the person using it. Having a professional kitchen doesn't make you a chef.

## Is It Worth the Price?

Semrush isn't cheap. The Pro plan starts at $139.95/month, with Guru at $249.95/month and Business at $499.95/month. In October 2025, Semrush also launched Semrush One, a bundled plan that pairs the core SEO toolkit with their new AI Visibility toolkit, starting at $199/month. For a small business, this is a real expense.

Here's how to think about it.

**It's worth it if:** You're actively doing SEO work (or paying someone to do it), you publish content regularly, you're in a competitive market, or you need to track your search performance over time. The data Semrush provides can directly inform decisions that generate revenue.

**It's probably not worth it if:** You have a simple brochure site that you rarely update, you're not investing in content or SEO, or you have a very limited budget that would be better spent on actual site improvements first.

**The middle ground:** Semrush offers a free tier with limited searches. Annual billing saves about 17%. There are also cheaper alternatives like Ubersuggest, SE Ranking, or Ahrefs (which is similarly priced but some people prefer). You don't need Semrush specifically. You need some source of SEO data.

## How to Get Value From It

If you do use Semrush, here's how to avoid wasting your subscription.

**Start with a site audit.** Fix the technical issues it finds before doing anything else. These are often quick wins that improve your rankings without creating new content.

**Research before you write.** Before creating any [blog post](/blog/how-to-write-seo-optimized-blog-posts/), check keyword volume and difficulty. Don't spend hours writing about a topic nobody searches for.

**Check competitors monthly.** See what new keywords they're ranking for. Look at their new content. Find the gaps they haven't covered yet. Keeping up with [current SEO trends and optimization shifts](/blog/seo-trends-that-actually-matter/) helps you know what to look for in the data.

**Track your progress.** Set up position tracking for your 20-30 most important keywords. Check monthly. Look for trends, not daily fluctuations.

**Review backlinks quarterly.** See what links you've gained and lost. Look for new linking opportunities. Disavow any toxic links.

## The Honest Take

Semrush is a powerful tool. It's also one of many. The SEO tools market is competitive, and several alternatives offer similar capabilities.

What matters more than which tool you use is whether you actually use it. A Semrush subscription you log into once a month is wasted money. A consistent SEO workflow backed by data from any decent tool will move your rankings.

The tool doesn't do the work. You do. Semrush just makes sure you're working on the right things.
