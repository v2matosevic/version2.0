# Riva Consulting: Website and Brand Identity for a Real Estate Firm

A real estate consulting firm with no visual identity is just a name on a business card. Riva Consulting needed more than a website. They needed an entire brand presence built from scratch.

## The Client

Riva Consulting specializes in real estate services and architectural visualization. They help clients navigate property decisions and bring building designs to life through detailed visual rendering. Their work is precise and professional. Their branding needed to say the same thing.

In real estate, first impressions decide whether a client picks up the phone. Visual credibility isn't a nice-to-have. It's the cost of entry. Riva Consulting understood this and came to us before they launched, not after.

## The Challenge

Starting from zero is both a challenge and an opportunity. No existing brand guidelines meant no constraints. But it also meant every decision, from the logo shape to the website color palette, needed to be made deliberately.

The design language had to communicate two things: credibility and sophistication. Real estate clients are making significant financial decisions. They need to trust the people advising them. That trust starts with how the company presents itself visually.

We needed to deliver a complete brand system. Logo, business cards, and a website that all speak the same language. Anything less and the professional image falls apart.

## What We Built

### Brand Identity

The logo draws direct inspiration from Riva Consulting's industry. The roofline shape symbolizes shelter and structure, two things their clients care about most. We iterated through multiple concepts before landing on this direction. The final mark is simple enough to work at any size (from a favicon to a building sign) while carrying enough personality to be memorable.

The typography is clean and readable, paired with a gold finish that positions the brand in the premium space. Gold was a deliberate choice. It communicates luxury and confidence without being flashy. Against deep black backgrounds, it creates a striking contrast that works across every touchpoint.

The business cards carry the same design language. Gold logo on deep black backgrounds. We used thicker card stock for a substantial feel in hand. Small detail, big impression. When someone hands you a Riva Consulting card, you remember it.

### The Website

We designed a clean, professional [website](/services/web-design/) that prioritizes usability and visual impact.

The layout uses the brand palette throughout: white backgrounds for content areas, black sections for emphasis, and gold accents for interactive elements and key details. The contrast creates clear visual hierarchy. Important information stands out. Navigation is intuitive so visitors can find property information, view the gallery, and get in touch without friction.

Key features include:

- **Google Maps integration** for property locations. Clients can see exactly where properties are and explore the surrounding area.
- **Filterable gallery** to showcase architectural visualization projects. Potential clients can browse by project type and see the quality of Riva Consulting's work before making contact.
- **Contact forms** for easy inquiry submission. Short forms, clear labels, no unnecessary fields. The goal is to make reaching out feel effortless.

The site is fully responsive and loads quickly on every device. We optimized images for fast loading without sacrificing the visual quality that an architectural visualization firm needs to demonstrate.

### Cohesive System

The real value of this project isn't any individual piece. It's how everything works together. The logo, the cards, the website. They all share the same design DNA. A potential client who receives a business card at a networking event and later visits the website sees the same brand. That consistency builds recognition and trust.

## The Result

Riva Consulting launched with a cohesive brand identity and a website that immediately communicates professionalism. Everything from the logo to the business cards to the site works together to build trust before a single meeting.

For a new company entering a competitive market, having a polished brand presence from day one makes the difference between looking established and looking like a startup figuring things out.
