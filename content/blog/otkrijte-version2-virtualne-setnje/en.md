# Version2 Virtual Tours: What They Are and Why They Work

Your website has photos. Nice ones, maybe. But photos are flat. They show one angle. One moment. One carefully chosen perspective that hides the awkward corner or the narrow hallway.

A 360 virtual tour shows everything. Every angle. Every room. Every detail. And the viewer controls what they see.

That's a fundamentally different experience. Here's why it matters and how Version2 makes it happen.

## What Is a 360 Virtual Tour?

A virtual tour is an interactive, navigable recreation of a physical space. The viewer sees a 360-degree view from any point in the space and can move between rooms, floors, or areas by clicking or tapping.

Think of it like Google Street View, but for interiors. Hotels, apartments, restaurants, offices, galleries, event venues. Any space that looks better when you can actually explore it.

The technology combines high-resolution 360-degree photography with specialized stitching software that blends the images into a continuous, navigable experience. The result loads in a browser. No app needed. Works on phones, tablets, and desktops.

For a deeper dive into the technology behind this, check out our explainer on [what virtual tours and 360 panoramas actually are](/blog/what-is-a-virtual-walk-and-360-panorama/).

## Why a Virtual Tour Beats a Photo Gallery

Photos tell. Tours show. Here's the practical difference:

**Space perception.** Photos can make a small room look big or a big room look cramped. A 360 tour gives accurate spatial understanding. Viewers develop a real sense of how rooms connect, how big they are, and how the space flows.

**Trust.** When you only show selected photos, potential customers wonder what you're not showing. A virtual tour hides nothing. That transparency builds confidence.

**Engagement.** People spend 5-10 times longer interacting with a virtual tour than scrolling through a photo gallery. More time on your page means more connection with your space.

**Reach.** An international buyer can tour a property in Zadar from their couch in Munich. A tourist in Tokyo can explore a hotel in Dubrovnik before booking. Geography stops being a barrier.

**Decision speed.** When people feel like they've already been somewhere, they decide faster. Real estate agents, hotel managers, and venue operators all report shorter decision cycles after adding virtual tours.

## Who Uses Virtual Tours?

### Real Estate

This is the biggest use case by far. [Virtual tours for real estate](/blog/virtual-tours-for-property-with-version2/) let buyers tour properties remotely, reduce unnecessary physical viewings, and attract international clients. Listings with virtual tours get significantly more engagement than those with photos alone.

Agents save time because only genuinely interested buyers schedule physical visits. Sellers get more exposure because the listing stands out.

### Hotels and Hospitality

A guest choosing between two similarly-priced hotels will pick the one where they can see the actual room, the pool area, the restaurant, and the lobby. A virtual tour removes the guesswork from booking.

Hotels in tourist-heavy areas like Croatia's coast can showcase their property to international guests who are comparing dozens of options online. The tour becomes a competitive advantage.

### Restaurants and Cafes

Ambiance matters. A lot. People don't just choose restaurants for the food. They choose them for the vibe, the seating, the view. A virtual tour lets potential guests experience the atmosphere before they book a table.

For event spaces within restaurants, this is especially powerful. Clients planning a wedding dinner or corporate event want to see the space. A virtual tour lets them explore it without driving across town.

### Museums and Cultural Venues

Accessibility is a real concern. Not everyone can visit physically. A virtual tour opens the space to people with mobility challenges, those in other cities, or students researching from home.

### Event Venues

Wedding planners, corporate event organizers, conference coordinators. They all need to evaluate spaces quickly. A virtual tour on your website lets them shortlist your venue without scheduling a visit.
