# SEO Checklist: 47 Things to Check Before You Launch

Checklists work because they turn overwhelming tasks into concrete steps. [SEO](/blog/what-is-seo-optimization/) has a lot of moving parts. Miss one, and you might never figure out why your rankings aren't improving.

This checklist covers everything. Technical foundations, on-page optimization, content, link building, local SEO, and e-commerce specifics. Work through it section by section. Check off what you've done. Fix what you haven't.

## Technical SEO Checklist

These are the foundations. If your technical SEO is broken, everything else you do is diminished.

**1. Google Search Console is set up and verified.** If you don't have this, stop and do it now. It's free, and it's your primary source of truth for how Google sees your site.

**2. XML sitemap is submitted.** Generate one with your CMS or a tool like Screaming Frog. Submit it in Search Console. Update it when you add or remove pages.

**3. Robots.txt is configured correctly.** Check that you're not accidentally blocking important pages. A single misplaced "Disallow" can hide your best content from Google.

**4. Site loads in under 3 seconds on mobile.** Test with PageSpeed Insights. Focus on Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), Interaction to Next Paint (INP), and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS). For specifics, see our [speed optimization guide](/blog/how-to-optimize-web-page-speed/).

**5. HTTPS is implemented site-wide.** No mixed content warnings. All HTTP URLs redirect to HTTPS.

**6. Mobile experience is tested on real devices.** Not just Chrome DevTools. Actual phones and tablets. Use Lighthouse to audit mobile usability and check Core Web Vitals for mobile-specific issues.

**7. No broken links (404 errors).** Use Screaming Frog or Ahrefs Site Audit to crawl your site. Fix or redirect any broken URLs.

**8. Clean URL structure.** Short, descriptive, lowercase. Hyphens between words. No query parameters in URLs that should be indexed.

**9. Canonical tags are implemented.** Every page should have a canonical tag pointing to its preferred URL. This prevents duplicate content issues.

**10. Structured data is in place.** At minimum: Organization schema on the homepage, Article schema on blog posts. Use Google's Rich Results Test to validate.

**11. No redirect chains.** Page A redirects to B, which redirects to C. Fix these so A goes directly to C. Each redirect in the chain wastes crawl budget and dilutes link equity.

**12. Core Web Vitals pass.** Check in Search Console under "Core Web Vitals" in the Experience section. Green is good. Fix anything orange or red.

## On-Page SEO Checklist

This is what's on your actual pages. Every important page on your site should pass these checks.

**13. Unique, descriptive title tag.** Under 60 characters. Includes the primary keyword near the beginning. Compelling enough to earn clicks.

**14. Meta description written for humans.** 150-160 characters. Summarizes the page content. Includes a reason to click. Not keyword-stuffed.

**15. One H1 tag per page.** It should clearly describe the page's main topic. Include the primary keyword naturally.

**16. Logical heading hierarchy.** H1 > H2 > H3. No skipping levels. Each heading should describe the section it introduces.

**17. [Primary keyword](/blog/what-are-the-keywords-for-what-they-serve/) appears naturally in the first 100 words.** Don't force it. But search engines pay attention to early content.

**18. Images are optimized.** WebP format where possible. Compressed file sizes. Descriptive alt text that includes relevant keywords naturally. Explicit width and height attributes to prevent layout shift.

**19. Internal links connect related content.** Every page should link to 2-5 other relevant pages on your site. Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here."

**20. Content matches search intent.** Search your target keyword. Does your page match the type and depth of content that's already ranking? If top results are 2,000-word guides and yours is a 200-word blurb, you know what to fix. Our guide on [SEO optimization for better search engine results](/blog/seo-optimization-for-better-searches-on-search-engines/) covers how to align content with what search engines reward.

**21. No thin content pages.** Every indexed page should provide substantial value. If a page has fewer than 300 words and no unique purpose, consider consolidating it with another page or noindexing it.

**22. No duplicate content.** Check for pages with identical or near-identical content. Consolidate with canonicals or redirects.

## Content Strategy Checklist

Creating content without a strategy is just creating noise.

**23. Target audience is defined.** Who are you writing for? What questions do they ask? What problems do they have? Write content that answers those specific questions.

**24. Keyword research is done with tools.** Use [SEMrush](/blog/what-is-semrush/), Ahrefs, or Google Keyword Planner. Don't guess what people search for. Look at the data.

**25. Content calendar exists.** Plan at least one month ahead. Consistency matters more than volume. One quality post per week beats five mediocre ones.

**26. Each post targets a specific keyword and intent.** No two pages should compete for the same keyword. That's called keyword cannibalization, and it hurts both pages.

**27. Content includes original insight.** Personal experience, unique data, expert opinions. Something an AI couldn't generate from public information alone.

**28. Content is updated regularly.** Set reminders to review and refresh key content every 6 months. Update statistics, add new information, improve sections that aren't performing.

**29. [Blog posts are SEO-optimized](/blog/how-to-write-seo-optimized-blog-posts/).** Proper heading structure, keyword placement, internal links, meta descriptions. Every single post.

**30. Content supports the buyer journey.** Have content for people at every stage. Awareness (educational posts), consideration (comparisons and guides), decision (case studies and service pages).

## Link Building Checklist

[Backlinks](/blog/what-are-backlinks-why-are-important/) remain one of Google's strongest ranking signals. Quality always beats quantity.

**31. Backlink profile is monitored.** Use Ahrefs or SEMrush to track who links to you, which links you've gained or lost, and what your competitors' link profiles look like.

**32. [Domain rating](/blog/what-is-domain-rating/) is tracked.** Know your baseline. Set realistic growth targets. Monitor monthly.

**33. Competitor backlinks are analyzed.** Find where your competitors get their links. Can you get links from the same or similar sources?

**34. Link-worthy content exists on the site.** Original research, tools, comprehensive guides, unique datasets. You need pages that other sites want to reference.

**35. Guest posting is part of the strategy.** Write for relevant industry publications. Not for link farms. Focus on sites with real audiences.

**36. Broken link building is being used.** Find broken outbound links on relevant sites. Offer your content as a replacement. Tools like Ahrefs' Broken Link Checker make this straightforward.

**37. Toxic links are disavowed.** If you see spammy sites linking to you (common if you've been around a while), use Google's Disavow Tool. Don't let other people's bad SEO hurt yours.

For a complete breakdown of link building strategies, read our [backlink acquisition guide](/blog/how-can-get-more-backlinks/).

## Local SEO Checklist

Essential for any business serving customers in a specific geographic area.

**38. [Google Business Profile](/blog/what-is-google-my-business/) is claimed and complete.** Every field filled. Business hours accurate. Categories selected. Description written.

**39. Photos are added regularly.** Interior, exterior, team, products/services. Fresh photos signal an active business.

**40. Reviews are being collected.** Ask customers after a successful project or purchase. Aim for a steady stream, not a burst. Respond to every review.

**41. NAP is consistent everywhere.** Your business Name, Address, and Phone must be identical across your website, Google Business Profile, directories, and social profiles.

**42. Local citations are built.** Get listed in relevant local and industry directories. Check for accuracy quarterly.

**43. Location pages exist (if multi-location).** Each location gets its own page with unique content. Not duplicated content with the city name swapped out.

## E-Commerce SEO Checklist

Additional items for online stores.

**44. Product pages have unique descriptions.** Not manufacturer copy that appears on 50 other sites. Write original descriptions that include relevant keywords and actually help the buyer decide.

**45. Category pages are optimized.** These are often your highest-value SEO pages. They should have introductory content, proper H1 tags, and internal links.

**46. Product schema markup is implemented.** Price, availability, reviews, ratings. This gets you rich results in search that increase click-through rates.

**47. Faceted navigation doesn't create index bloat.** If filtering creates hundreds of nearly identical URLs, use canonical tags or noindex to prevent Google from wasting crawl budget on them.

## How to Use This Checklist

Don't try to do everything at once. Prioritize like this:

1. **Technical issues first.** If Google can't crawl and index your site properly, nothing else matters.
2. **On-page optimization for your top 10 pages.** These are likely driving most of your current traffic. Make them better.
3. **Content gaps.** Where are you missing content that your competitors have?
4. **Link building.** This is ongoing. Start with the easy wins (directories, broken link building) and build toward earned media.
5. **Local SEO.** If applicable, this often delivers the fastest visible results.

Come back to this checklist quarterly. SEO isn't something you finish. It's something you maintain. Staying current with [new trends in SEO optimization](/blog/new-trends-in-seo-optimization-what-you-need-to-know/) ensures your checklist evolves as the landscape shifts.
