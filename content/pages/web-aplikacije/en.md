# Beyond websites. Full applications.

A website shows information. A web application does work. It processes data, manages users, handles payments, runs business logic. If you need something that thinks, not just something that sits there, you need a web app.

We build them from scratch. No low-code platforms. No drag-and-drop builders that fall apart when requirements get real.

## What We Build

### Client Portals

Login, role-based access, personalized dashboards. Your customers or team members see exactly what they need and nothing they don't. Content access, document sharing, profile management.

### Admin Dashboards

Real-time data, charts, tables, filters. The control panel your business runs on. Connected to your database, your APIs, your payment systems. Not a spreadsheet with a pretty face.

### Booking and Scheduling Systems

Calendar views, availability management, automated confirmations, payment collection. For clinics, consultants, rental businesses, anyone who manages appointments.

### SaaS Tools

Your product idea, built as a web application. Multi-tenant architecture, subscription billing, user management. From prototype to production.

### Event Management Platforms

Registration, ticketing, attendee tracking, check-in systems. Everything from a local meetup to a multi-day conference.

### Member Portals

Content libraries, progress tracking, community features, journals. Membership businesses need more than a login page. They need an experience that keeps people coming back.

## When You Need a Web App

Not everything needs to be a web application. But if your project involves any of these, it does:

- **User accounts** with different permission levels
- **Real-time data** that changes and needs to be reflected instantly
- **Complex workflows** with multiple steps, approvals, or status changes
- **Integrations** with payment systems, third-party APIs, or internal tools
- **Data processing** that goes beyond storing and displaying content

If you're trying to do any of this with WordPress plugins or a website builder, you've already outgrown those tools.
