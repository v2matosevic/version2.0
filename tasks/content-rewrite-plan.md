## 1. Editorial Quick Reference

### Voice
A sharp friend who happens to be really good at building things for the web. Confident but not arrogant. Technical but not jargon-heavy. Direct but not cold. Slightly edgy. We have opinions.

### Banned Words
Never use: innovative, cutting-edge, solutions, leverage, empower, seamless, transform, elevate, next-level, holistic, synergy, robust, dynamic

### Also Avoid
- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "Contact us today to learn more"
- "Your trusted partner"
- "We are dedicated to..."
- "Exceed expectations"
- Any sentence that could appear on any agency's website

### Formatting
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Short sentences. Break them apart.
- No em dashes
- No semicolons in copy
- Headlines: punchy and opinionated, not descriptive

### Consistent Data Points
These must be verified with user and used consistently across ALL pages:
- Founded: September 2022
- Location: Zadar, Croatia
- Team: Small, in-house. No outsourcing.
- Price positioning: Premium. Typical projects €5,000+, minimum €2,500 for basic landing pages.
- Digital card stats: 486+ users, 4.94 rating
- Smart Web Plan: from €30/month
- **Project count: 100+** (confirmed by owner — as a company, not per developer)
- **Client count: 100+** (confirmed by owner)

### Service Terminology
**USE:** custom websites, web applications, online stores, e-commerce, AI integration, digital business cards, 360° virtual tours, SEO, integrations, maintenance
**NEVER as standalone services:** social media management, video production, photography, PPC/paid advertising
**WordPress:** Acknowledge as existing platform clients use. Don't promote it. Don't trash it. Position custom development as the better path forward.

## 4. Blog Post Rewrite Guidelines

### Structure Template

```markdown
---
title: "Compelling Title (can differ from original)"
slug: "keep-existing-slug"
(keep all other frontmatter, update category to new mapping)
---

# [Engaging headline that hooks the reader]

[Opening: 1-2 sentences. State the problem or question. Make them care.]

## [First major section]

[Content: practical, specific, opinionated per brand voice]

## [More sections as needed]

[Each section: 2-4 paragraphs. Include examples, tools, numbers.]

## [Final section]

[Wrap up with value. No "in conclusion." No generic closing.]

---

*[Soft CTA relating to post topic. Link to relevant service page or /analysis/.]*
```

### Rewrite Rules

1. **Kill every banned word.** Search and destroy across all 103 posts.
2. **Remove dropped service references.** No social media management, video production, photography, PPC as services we offer.
3. **WordPress positioning.** Don't trash it. Acknowledge it. Position custom as the better path. "If you're on WordPress, here's how. And here's when custom makes more sense."
4. **Internal links.** 2-4 per post where natural. Link to related blog posts and service pages.
5. **No generic openers.** Never start with "In today's digital world" or any variation.
6. **No generic closers.** Never end with "Contact us to learn more." The CTA is separated from content.
7. **Be specific.** Tools, numbers, examples. Not vague claims.
8. **Target length.** 800-1500 words. Expand posts under 600. Tighten posts over 2000.
9. **One CTA at the end.** Separated by a horizontal rule. Relevant to the post topic.
10. **Frontmatter updates.** Update category to match curation manifest. Improve title/excerpt if needed. Keep slug, dates, translations unchanged.
